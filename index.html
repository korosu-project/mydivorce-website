Step-by-Step Divorce Process

We’ll break down the process in simple, actionable steps.

Step-by-Step Divorce Process

  1. Step 1: Understand Your Type of Divorce
    Decide whether you want to file for a Mutual Consent Divorce or a Contested Divorce depending on your situation.
  2. Step 2: Hire a Lawyer (Optional but Recommended)
    A lawyer can guide you through the paperwork, filing, and court appearances.
  3. Step 3: Prepare Required Documents
    Gather documents like your Marriage Certificate, ID proofs, address proofs, income/asset details, and others.
  4. Step 4: File the Divorce Petition in Court
    Submit the petition in the family court of the appropriate jurisdiction.
  5. Step 5: Attend Counseling or Mediation (if applicable)
    The court may refer you to counseling or mediation to try reconciliation.
  6. Step 6: Court Hearings
    Appear before the judge, present your case (in contested divorce), or attend scheduled hearings in mutual consent divorce.
  7. Step 7: Cooling-off Period (Mutual Consent Only)
    A 6-month period is mandatory before the second motion is filed. In some cases, it can be waived.
  8. Step 8: Final Decree of Divorce
    After both parties agree and legal formalities are done, the judge grants the final divorce decree.

Documents Required

  • Marriage Certificate
  • ID Proof of both parties
  • Address Proof
  • Income and asset details (if applicable)
